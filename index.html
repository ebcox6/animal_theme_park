Animal-Themed Theme Park Project

Welcome, 6th Grade Theme Park Designers! Get ready for an exciting adventure! Imagine you’ve just been hired as a superstar design team for the coolest amusement park company on the planet. Your mission is to create the wildest, most amazing animal-themed amusement park ever! But there's a twist—you need to make it all about celebrating the incredible animals and habitats of one of Earth’s continents, while also inspiring visitors to help protect these creatures and their homes. Your park will be full of fun, thrills, and learning—where every ride, habitat, and snack stand has a story to tell!

The Challenge

You and your group will be assigned a continent to represent in your theme park. Your goal is to design a park that highlights the incredible animals, their natural habitats, and the importance of conserving these habitats. Each group will create an exhibit that includes:

  • Animal Attraction/Habitat: Create a model of an animal habitat or attraction that features animals from your continent. This could be a rainforest, savanna, tundra, or any natural environment where the animals live.
  • Themed Amusement Ride: Design an exciting ride inspired by the animals or environments of your continent. Imagine the thrill of zooming through a jungle or flying over mountains!
  • Concession Stand: Invent a themed concession stand that offers snacks inspired by the continent. Think of interesting names or foods that could connect to the animals or culture of the area.
  • Souvenir Shop: Create a souvenir shop that features items to help guests remember their adventure, while also promoting conservation. You could include educational posters, plush animals, or even recycled-material gifts.

Your Final Product

Your group will create a trifold board that will be displayed during our "Theme Park Science Fair." The board should include:

  • Information about your assigned continent.
  • Details about the animals that live there and the conservation efforts needed to protect them.
  • Pictures, drawings, and fun facts to make your board interesting and engaging.

Additionally, you will build a diorama or shadow box (like using a shoebox) that shows one section of your theme park. This could be the animal habitat, the ride, the concession stand, or the souvenir shop. Your group’s diorama will be part of a larger park map that includes all the continents and their attractions!

Requirements for Completion

  • Research: Gather information about the animals, habitats, and conservation needs of your assigned continent.
  • Creativity: Be creative in your designs! Use bright colors, interesting shapes, and clever ideas to make your theme park stand out.
  • Collaboration: Work well as a team. Every member should contribute their ideas and skills to create a successful project.
  • Presentation: Be ready to present your theme park to your classmates. Practice explaining your design choices, the animals you chose, and why conservation is important.

Project Benchmarks

Benchmark 1: Research & Brainstorm (End of Week 2)

Target: Complete initial research on your assigned continent.

Requirements: Gather information about the animals, their habitats, and conservation efforts. Brainstorm ideas for each of the key areas (habitat, ride, concession stand, souvenir shop).

Completion Check: Submit a summary of your research and initial sketches or notes of your ideas.

Benchmark 2: Design & Draft (End of Week 4)

Target: Complete the design phase for your trifold board and start creating your diorama.

Requirements: Draft the layout for the trifold board, including main sections like animals, habitats, and conservation. Start building the basic structure of your diorama.

Completion Check: Present your trifold board draft and initial diorama to the teacher for feedback.

Benchmark 3: Build & Refine (End of Week 6)

Target: Finalize the diorama and refine your trifold board based on feedback.

Requirements: Complete all physical elements—diorama, trifold board, and group presentation script. Ensure all content is accurate and visually engaging.

Completion Check: Participate in a practice presentation session to refine communication skills.

Benchmark 4: Presentation Day (Week 7)

Target: Present your theme park during our "Theme Park Science Fair."

Requirements: Set up your trifold board and diorama, and prepare to present your work to classmates and visitors. Be ready to answer questions and explain your conservation focus.

Completion Check: Deliver a well-prepared, engaging presentation and answer audience questions confidently.

Guiding Questions

  • What makes the animals on your continent unique?
  • How can your theme park teach visitors about the importance of protecting animal habitats?
  • What creative ways can you make your theme park fun while educating guests about conservation?
  • How can you use colors, sounds, and visuals to make your park attractions even more exciting?
  • If you could choose one animal as the “mascot” of your theme park, which one would it be and why?
  • How can your souvenir shop encourage visitors to support conservation efforts?
  • What would be the coolest name for your amusement ride and why does it fit the theme of your continent?
